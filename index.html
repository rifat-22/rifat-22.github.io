About

I'm a second-year PhD student in the Computer Science and Engineering Department at the University of North Texas, working under Dr. Mark Albert in the Biomedical AI Lab. My research interests lie in health informatics, clinical NLP, applied machine learning, and bioinformatics, with a focus on turning unstructured clinical notes into structured signal for patient care. Before this, I spent three and a half years as a software engineer at Dynamic Solution Innovators Limited and Inovace Technologies Limited in Dhaka, Bangladesh.

Publications

Pain Severity Classification from Electronic Health Records in Children with Cerebral Palsy

The first systematic benchmark for classifying pain severity in children with cerebral palsy from real-world clinical notes, comparing several natural language processing and language-model approaches to recover clinically meaningful signal from everyday documentation.

Submitted to JMIR Medical Informatics · 2026 · DOI

Instruction Fine-Tuned Language Model for Pediatric Fall Risk Assessment via Iterative Training Using Generated Clinical Texts

This study introduces the first language-model approach to fall risk assessment in children with cerebral palsy. It turns unstructured clinical notes into clinically meaningful risk signals. The approach relies on validated training methods designed for settings with limited labeled data.

IEEE ICHI 2026 · Nov 2025

Hybrid Ensemble of Segmentation-Assisted Classification and GBDT for Skin Cancer Detection with Engineered Metadata and Synthetic Lesions from ISIC 2024 Non-Dermoscopic 3D-TBP Images

Hybrid deep learning and GBDT ensemble for skin cancer detection using 3D-TBP images and synthetic lesion augmentation.
